ABOUT

A flagship platform for applied urban research

MOVE System is developed by the Institute of Smart City and Management (ISCM) at the University of Economics Ho Chi Minh City (UEH). Its core objectives are to conduct applied research in road safety, urban mobility, rail systems, and low-carbon transition.

The System supports project-based education and develops fit-for-purpose technological solutions to generate robust, evidence-based insights that directly support decision-making and policymaking at city and national levels.

MOVE advances traffic safety, net-zero emissions, and smart city development in line with UEH's 2025–2030 Strategy, strengthening UEH's role as a leading University-City Innovation Hub in Asia.

LAB · 01 / SEE

SEE Living Lab

A pioneering platform designed to strengthen road safety for road users in Vietnam — through gamification, immersive VR, eye-tracking, and machine learning.

P1 · Sense

R2S Education

Virtual platform. Enhance traffic rules knowledge, situation-awareness, risk detection and management.

P2 · Engage

R2S VR Cycling

Hybrid platform. Improve hazard-perception skills and virtual cycling behavior.

P3 · Evolve

R2S Map

Virtual platform. Map and visualize safe routes for vulnerable road users.

P4 · Extension

Spatial Problem–Solution Mapping

Virtual platform. Support decision making and policy design.

LAB · 02 / SML

Smart Mobility Lab

Advancing intelligent transportation systems and sustainable urban mobility — MaaS, smart routing, traffic operations, and last-mile micro-mobility.

THEME 01

MaaS & Smart Routing

Real-time transportation platform, integrated payments, transparency data and demand modelling informed by urban structure.

THEME 02

Smart Mobility & Traffic Operation

Adaptive signal control, AI demand forecasting, route optimization for HCMC's mixed traffic.

THEME 03

Last-Mile & Micro-Mobility

Micro-mobility hub/stop planning, data efficiency and small-vehicle safety.

CE-M Program — Campus EV Micro-Mobility

A real-world testbed for electric and shared mobility: e-motorbike + e-shuttle bus network, campus-first incentives, and partnerships with fleet suppliers and charging providers.

LAB · 03 / RAIL

CE-Rail@UEH

A dedicated railway laboratory building capacity in modern rail systems through small-scale simulation testbeds and a rail–city smart corridor living lab.

SPACE 01

Rail Systems Lab

Rolling stock mock-up, signalling simulator, PTC/CBTC training modules, and SCADA supervisory control sandbox for hands-on training and certification.

SPACE 02

Rail-City Smart Corridor Living Lab

TOD prototypes, passenger-flow experiments, and last-mile integration with e-mobility and micro-mobility. Inspired by collaborative programs like Tokyo Metro.

LAB · 04 / CTR

CtrLab — Carbon Transition Lab

Understand, quantify, and control carbon emissions and their impacts through data-driven science — integrating emissions, exposure, energy and climate into one unified system.

AIR

Air Quality

PM2.5 spatial distribution, urban morphology interactions and exposure-risk maps via WRF-CMAQ.

CLIMATE

Climate Change Adaptation

Integrated urban digital twin linking transportation, pollution and urban heat.

ENERGY

Energy Efficiency

Long-term analysis of electricity consumption and economic drivers of intensity turning points.
